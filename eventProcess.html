Golden Gate Events Poster Redesign

Project Overview

Golden Gate Park withholds numerous amount of events that go on throughout its location. Expanding more than 3 miles east to west, this historical landmark crowns itself as the 3rd most visited park in the United States. On the month of October, the park held 18 events that went on throughout its respective sub-locations. In order to advertise the vast amount of celebrations that went forth, an event poster was illustrated to showcase the dates, times, and locations of the events that went on in the Month. Using abstract shape and type, the goal of this project was to create a strong composition while still adhering to Golden Gate Park’s identity and brand.

The intention of this project was to take inspiration from modernist principles and implement it into a fully realized composition. Type layout, type setting, strong grid structure, and modern design principles were all considered when creating the Golden Gate Park Event Poster. Creating this composition taught myself the necessity of ideations and iterations, and the creative potential of type and layout.

Ideations

First Round

The first round of ideations was composed of text layout and using typography as background. The goal was to use modernist typographic elements and strong grid structure to create a substantial composition.

Second Round

The second round of ideations consisted of using simple shapes to subtly represent the idea of fall. Trees were illustrated through the triangles as means to mimic Golden Gate Park's setting and atmosphere, without intruding on the theme on its specific events.

Iterations

The chosen iteration became a combination of many specific elements from the compositions; the chaos of geometric shapes, triangular slice along the title, vertical typography, and mountainous reflection were utilized to emphasize the definition of fall. Set times were added at the end of each description to allow the reader to read seamlessly through the dated events. Orphans and widows were also taken into account among the iterations

Final Iterations

Although the type hierarchy was legible for the reader, the alignment along the event's grid was rigid and forced. This structure left no room for negative space and leading along the vertical columns. As a solution, it was decided that breaking the structure to create negative space was needed to allow an organic flow. The addition of the textured triangle was also implemented in order to create a focus point for the user to relax among the chaos of the shapes.

Conclusion

The final iteration utilizes modernist principles in order to abstractly represent Golden Gate Park Events. Using the typeface Interstate, the typesetting maintains a consistent rag while clearly identifying titles, dates, descriptions, and times. This process taught myself the importance of ideations and iterations, and the creative potential of type and layout.
