TAZO Package Redesign

Project Overview

Insomniac Events is a venue company that holds one of the largest music festivals in the entire world. With thousands of DJ's who perform at its raves, it is hard to keep track of every artist without its app. The Insomniac app is mostly used to track DJs and save festivals for the user. However, its journey and interface is in need of easier function. The redesign that was implemented simplifies the user path, while also increasing potential for user retention and usability.

Introduction

History dates back to ancient China. Grew rapidly from the 4th through the 8th century. Tea has a deep history of culture and lifestyle. Tea had also been known as a status symbol, only the wealthy could afford tea. The history of tea is more traditional and of a lifestyle, compared to coffee. According to myjapanesegreentea, tea is a ritual, and could be used for meditation and mindfulness. Many tea-ist believe that tea enriches life emotionally, not physically. Tea is known to be soothing and ritualistic; an activity rather than something to drink.

Premise

TAZO is a highly reputable tea company that identifies itself among consumers who seek adventure and unique experiences. TAZO uses naturistic colors and a light color scheme to mimic its overall goals and brand message. It defines itself as the ‘unexpected’ tea brand - an outlier of its kind.

How can TAZO refresh its modern, minimalist brand while still incorporating a nostalgic and traditional notion of tea? How could we invite new tea drinkers into the brand while still maintaining the fact that TAZO is a part of the culture of herbal tea? The company’s brand direction could split in two ways:

Increase the amount of young-trendy caffeine drinkers by capitalizing on its modern aesthetic.
Expand its target audience to other typical tea drinkers by implementing traditional historical elements of tea packaging.

Ideation and Moodboarding

Because TAZO differentiates itself by adding a sense of modernity and commercialism, I aim to continue this typographic focus. However, I want TAZO to take a ‘step back’ to the lifestyle and culture of tea, by adding subtle elements like typeface choice texture, and less imagery. By using san serif and modernist layout, I want to capture the emotion of the city and the feel of nature.

Sketches

The main focus of my sketches was creating a dynamic typographic layouts. I wanted the main focus of the imagery to be used with vertical and horizontal type layout. Strong grid structure was also an important aspect of the work.

Iterations

First Iteration

Type exploration was the first step into creating the composition. Trial and error with different typefaces was implemented in order to find a Serif-typeface with a variations of fonts. After different ideation and implementations, the typeface Kepler STD was chosen for its versatility and amount of choice (over 30 fonts).

Second Iteration

The second iteration intialized simple imagery and gradient. The intention was to combine the smooth gradient along with the serif for elegant movement. However, the type layout combated too much with the modernist direction, therefore removing the composition's consistency.

Third Iteration

In order to find ways to maintain its modernist principles, images in the shape of boxes were iterated to satisfy the work. However the image of the composition, hibiscus leaves, held too much representational concept to the TAZO box. This also combated the direction of the work.

Creating Imagery

In order to implement texture in a more abstract manner, images of plants and materials were taken and transferred onto Illustrator. Image trace was used to manipulate and revitalized the texture as an abstract piece, while still maintaining the foundations of natural leaves.
